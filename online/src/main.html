ABOUT
ENTRADOX
A multi-faceted team with expertise in mechanics, circuits and wiring, programming, arts, promotion, outreach and more. Our 10 member strong team works to strategize, build, program and compete with select robots. Outreach is also a major focus, in which we teach and collaborate with youth, other FTC Teams around the globe and community members.



In 2018, Meadowridge school initiated the FTC robotics program. As a rookie team, the team struggled to construct a reliable robot. Nevertheless, they learned from more experienced teams and grew into what became known as Blown Fuses Forever.



In the following season, the team rebranded to Team BFF (Blown Fuses Forever). Having grown from the previous season, BFF produced a rugged robot, reliably scoring points in major tournaments. In the 2019-20 season, BFF ranked and performed at the league average.

In preparation for the new season, our members decided that with our new team we should have a new brand identity. With that, what was previously known as the Blown Fuses Forever (BFFs) became Entradox Robotics. This summer was the end to a beginning for this team; many of our members met before the start of school to disassemble last year's robot, Joe-E. It was emotional, marking the end of the BFFs, but the disassembly process inspired ideas for this new season of challenges.

My name is Akira Yoshiyama and I am currently in 11th grade at Meadowridge School in British Columbia, Canada. This is my first FTC season, but third season participating in robotics events. Previously, I competed in the French Robotique Zone01 and WRO in Port Coquitlam, BC. I am the coding team manager (with website development on the side), however, I also work on robot design and building. Outside of robotics I enjoy programming, debate, running and biking, and learning math.

2024 ROBOT
ANASTAISIA
2024 ROBOT
ANASTAISIA is a robot developed by Entradox Robotics. This is designed for the 2023-2024 CENTERSTAGE.

The design includes mecanum wheels, which turn through chains connected to our motors.

It has a grabber arm extruding from the base to transfor peices onto the board, and a hanging arm to hang onto the bar.

At the center there is also a projectile propeller, and the robot is incased in clear glass because it's hot.
